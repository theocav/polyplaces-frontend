Care Guide

Displaying your sculpture

Your sculpture looks best displayed on a flat surface away from direct sunlight. Prolonged UV exposure can cause the matte white surface to yellow slightly over time. A shelf, mantlepiece, or desk out of direct sun is ideal.

Avoid humid environments (such as bathrooms) which may cause warping over an extended period.

Cleaning

Dust occasionally with a soft, dry brush or a can of compressed air. The fine relief detail can trap dust in narrow valleys — a soft paintbrush works well for this.

Do not use cleaning sprays or harsh chemicals on the sculpture surface. The matte finish is porous and liquids may stain or weaken the material.

Handling

Handle your sculpture by the base or edges where the relief is shallowest. Avoid pressing on peaks or fine details as these are the most delicate parts of the piece.

Storage

If you need to store your sculpture, return it to its original box. Pack any gaps with soft tissue paper to prevent movement during storage or transit.

Questions?

If you have a concern about your piece, get in touch and we’ll be happy to help.
